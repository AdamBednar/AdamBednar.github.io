Dhillon making big promises in Baltimore, but can he deliver?

By: Adam Bednar Daily Record Business Writer

Kahan S. Dhillon Jr., 37, a stocky man with a neatly trimmed beard and wearing a black pin-striped suit and a black turban, arrived at Capital Grille on Pratt Street in downtown Baltimore with a joke prepared.

It was a muggy July afternoon, and Dhillon, a Sikh, was quick to quip about the heat and the turban making it worse. The joke, and one other made during an hour-long interview, were tossed off nonchalantly, as if second nature to a man who regularly feels the need to put at ease people not familiar with his faith.

But in Dhillon’s case it’s not his headwear that’s drawing attention in Baltimore. It’s the $10 billion in investment he claims he can bring to the city that’s turning heads.

“Right now, Baltimore is at the bottom of the barrel.
It’s time to get it to being at the top, it’s time to get it to where it’s potential is, which is at the front of the line …
I always like to say it took a lot for Baltimore to get where it is now, and it’s going to take a lot more to get it out,”
— Dhillon said.
